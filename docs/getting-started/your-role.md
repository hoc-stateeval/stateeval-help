# Understanding Your Role in eVAL

Your role in eVAL determines what you can see and do in the system. You may have one or more roles depending on your position.

:::tip Multiple Roles
Many users have multiple roles. For example, a principal is typically both an **evaluator** (evaluating teachers) and an **evaluatee** (being evaluated by district staff). Use the work area selector to switch between your different roles.
:::

## Administrative Roles

Administrative roles manage staff and configure eVAL settings. These users don't participate in evaluations directly.

### District Administrator

**What you can do:**
- Manage all staff across the entire district
- Add, edit, and remove staff members at any school
- Upload district-wide staff rosters
- Assign evaluators to principals and teachers
- Configure district evaluation settings
- View reports across all schools

**Your work area:** District Admin

### School Administrator

**What you can do:**
- Manage staff at your school
- Add and edit staff members at your school
- Assign evaluators to teachers at your school
- Send account invitations to staff
- View reports for your school

**Your work area:** School Admin

### District Assignment Manager

**What you can do:**
- Assign evaluators to teachers (principals evaluating teachers)
- Assign district evaluators to principals
- View assignment status across the district

This is a focused role for users who only need to manage evaluation assignments without full administrative access.

### District Viewer

**What you can do:**
- View evaluator accounts and their progress
- Select a principal and view their evaluation work
- Monitor evaluation completion across schools

This is a read-only role for oversight without the ability to make changes.

## Evaluation Roles

Evaluation roles participate in the evaluation processâ€”either as an evaluator, an evaluatee, or both.

### Who Evaluates Whom?

| If you are a... | You are evaluated by... |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Teacher** | Principal at your school, or a District Teacher Evaluator |
| **Assistant Principal** | Head Principal at your school, or a District Principal Evaluator |
| **Head Principal** | District Principal Evaluator |

### Teacher

**As an evaluatee, you can:**
- Complete self-assessments
- Submit artifacts demonstrating your practice
- View observation notes and feedback
- Track your evaluation progress
- View your final evaluation results

**Your work area:** Teacher

**Who evaluates you:** Usually your school's principal. In some cases (such as specialists or special education teachers), you may be evaluated by a District Teacher Evaluator.

### Principal

Principals typically have **two roles**: evaluator and evaluatee.

**As an evaluator, you can:**
- Conduct observations of teachers at your school
- Review teacher artifacts and evidence
- Complete mid-year and final evaluations for teachers
- Provide feedback throughout the year

**As an evaluatee, you can:**
- Complete your own self-assessment
- Submit artifacts demonstrating your leadership
- View feedback from your evaluator
- Track your own evaluation progress

**Your work areas:** Principal Evaluator (for evaluating) and Principal (for your own evaluation)

**Who evaluates you:** A District Principal Evaluator, or (for assistant principals) your school's Head Principal.

### Head Principal

At schools with multiple principals, the Head Principal has additional responsibilities.

**As an evaluator, you can:**
- Evaluate teachers at your school
- Evaluate assistant principals at your school

**As an evaluatee:**
- You are evaluated by a District Principal Evaluator

### District Principal Evaluator (DPE)

**What you can do:**
- Evaluate principals across the district
- Conduct observations of principals
- Complete principal evaluations
- View and manage your assigned principals

**Your work area:** District Principal Evaluator

### District Teacher Evaluator (DTE)

**What you can do:**
- Evaluate teachers across the district (typically specialists)
- Conduct observations of assigned teachers
- Complete teacher evaluations

This role is typically for evaluating teachers with specialized training that school principals may not be equipped to evaluate.

**Your work area:** District Teacher Evaluator

## Finding Your Role

Not sure what role you have?

1. Log in to eVAL
2. Look at the **work area selector** in the top navigation
3. The available work areas show your assigned roles

If you believe you're missing a role or have the wrong role, contact your district administrator.
