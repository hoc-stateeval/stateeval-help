# How to Complete a Self-Assessment

This guide walks an evaluatee through creating and sharing a self-assessment. For the concepts behind it, see [Self-Assessment](../reference/self-assessment.md).

## 1. Create the Self-Assessment — *Setup* tab

1. Go to **Self-Assessment** and create a new one.
2. On the **Setup** tab, give it a title.
3. It opens in **Draft** — private to you until you share it.

## 2. Reflect and Align Evidence — *Evidence View* tab

1. Open the **Evidence View** to work against the rubric.
2. Add evidence and align it to the rubric components that reflect your practice.
3. If your district allows **self-assessment scoring**, record your self-scores. These are for your own reflection — they don't contribute to your summative score.

## 3. Add Artifacts — *Artifacts* tab (optional)

Attach supporting documents as embedded artifacts. Share them before you share the self-assessment, or they won't be included.

## 4. Share With Your Evaluator — *Report* tab

1. Review your self-assessment on the **Report** tab.
2. When you're ready, **share** it from the Report tab. It becomes read-only and visible to your evaluator.

## Making Changes After Sharing

A shared self-assessment is read-only, but you can **Unlock** it yourself to make changes — no evaluator approval needed — then share it again.
