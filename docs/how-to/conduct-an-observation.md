# How to Conduct an Observation

This guide walks an evaluator through an observation from start to finish, tab by tab. For the concepts behind observations, see [Observations](../reference/observations.md).

## Before You Start

- Make sure you're in the correct **work area** — **Evaluate Teachers** or **Evaluate Principals** — for the location where you're observing. If you evaluate at more than one school, switch to the right one first. See [Navigating Work Areas](../getting-started/work-areas.md).
- Confirm the evaluatee has an evaluator and plan type assigned — see [Assignments & Plan Types](../reference/assignments.md).
- If you'll use conference prompts, have them ready in your Prompt Bank — see [Using Prompts](../reference/prompts.md).

## 1. Set Up the Observation — *Setup* tab

1. Go to **Observations** and create a new observation.
2. On the **Setup** tab, enter a **title**, choose the **observation type** (Informal or Formal), and set the **observation date, time, and duration**.
3. Turn on **Pre-Conference** and/or **Post-Conference** if you'll use them, and set their dates and times.
4. Optionally, check **Exclude from Summative Report** if this observation shouldn't be included.

The observation opens in **Draft**. It's visible to the evaluatee right away, but your **Evidence View** and **Report** tabs stay hidden from them until you share those tabs or submit.

## 2. Set Up Conference Prompts — *Setup* tab

When a conference is enabled, a **Conference Prompts** panel appears on the Setup tab (with **Pre-Conference** and **Post-Conference** sub-tabs when both are on). There you can:

- select prompts from your Prompt Bank,
- add a local prompt just for this observation, and
- send the prompts to the evaluatee.

The evaluatee is notified and responds. See [Using Prompts](../reference/prompts.md).

## 3. Observe and Capture Evidence — *Observe & Code* tab

The **Observe & Code** tab is your working space during and after the observation:

- Write your **Observation Notes**.
- Review the evaluatee's **pre- and post-conference responses** as they share them.
- Add **conference summaries** and share them if you'd like.

Select passages in your notes or the evaluatee's responses and [code them as evidence](../evidence/text-coding.md) aligned to rubric components.

## 4. Add Artifacts — *Artifacts* tab (optional)

Attach supporting documents as embedded artifacts. Share them before you submit, or they won't be included in the published evidence.

## 5. Review, Package, and Score — *Evidence View* tab

1. Review all aligned evidence organized against the rubric.
2. Group related evidence into [evidence packages](../evidence/evidence-packages.md) and align them to specific rubric performance-level descriptors.
3. If your district allows observation scoring, assign rubric component scores.
4. Check the **Report** tab to preview the report.

## 6. Send the Final Report

1. When you're ready, send the final report (Formal mode) or submit/lock it (Simple mode).
2. In **Formal** mode, the evaluatee reviews and **acknowledges** the report before it locks. In **Simple** mode, it locks immediately and the evaluatee is notified.
3. Once locked, the observation's evidence becomes public and flows to the Summative — see [Observations](../reference/observations.md#how-it-flows-to-the-summative).

## Making Changes After Locking

A locked observation can't be edited directly. Either party can **request an unlock**, which the other must approve; or, before the evaluatee acknowledges, the evaluator can revert it to **Draft**. See [How Workflows Work](../reference/workflows.md#unlock-requests-mutual-approval).
