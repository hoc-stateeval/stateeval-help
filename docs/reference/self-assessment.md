# Self-Assessment

A self-assessment is the evaluatee's own reflection on their practice against the evaluation rubric. It's a **creator-managed** evidence collection: the evaluatee owns it, works on it privately, and shares it with their evaluator when ready.

This page explains what a self-assessment is and how it works. For the step-by-step process, see [How to Complete a Self-Assessment](../how-to/complete-a-self-assessment.md).

## What It's For

A self-assessment is a reflection tool. The evaluatee reviews the rubric, considers where their practice stands, aligns supporting evidence, and — if the district allows — records self-scores. It helps frame the conversation with the evaluator early in the cycle.

:::info Reflection Only — Not Part of the Summative Score
Unlike observations and student growth, a self-assessment's evidence and any self-scores **do not flow to the Summative Evaluation**. It's shared with the evaluator for context, but it doesn't contribute to the final performance rating.
:::

## Creator-Managed Workflow

Self-assessments don't use Simple/Formal completion modes. The evaluatee manages the lifecycle directly:

| State | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| **Draft** | The evaluatee is working on it; it's private to the evaluatee. |
| **Shared** | The evaluatee has shared it with the evaluator. It becomes read-only — but the evaluatee can **Unlock** it to make changes and then share again. |

Sharing is done from the **Report** tab. The evaluator can *view* a shared self-assessment but doesn't score or approve it.

## What's in a Self-Assessment

| Tab | What's There |
|-----|--------------|
| **Setup** | Title and basic setup |
| **Evidence View** | Reflect against the rubric, align evidence, and self-score (if your district allows self-assessment scoring) |
| **Artifacts** | Attach supporting documents as embedded artifacts |
| **Report** | Preview the self-assessment and share it with your evaluator when ready |
