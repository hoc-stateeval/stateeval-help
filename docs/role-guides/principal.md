# Principal Guide

As a principal, you play **two roles** in eVAL at the same time, and you switch between them using [Work Areas](../getting-started/work-areas.md):

- **Evaluator** — you evaluate the teachers at your school (and, if you're a Head Principal, usually the assistant principals too).
- **Evaluatee** — you have your own annual evaluation, conducted by a District Principal Evaluator (DPE).

Because of that, the two existing guides both apply to you:

| Your Role | Use This Guide | What It Covers |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| Evaluating your staff | [Evaluator Guide](./evaluator.md) | Observations, evidence, scoring, prompts, workflows |
| Your own evaluation | [Evaluatee Guide](./evaluatee.md) | Self-assessment, artifacts, student growth, reviewing feedback |

## Two Hats, Two Setups

Each role depends on its own setup being complete, and they're independent:

- You can start evaluating your teachers as soon as the **Teacher** framework is loaded and their evaluations are set up — even if your own evaluation isn't ready yet.
- Your own evaluation becomes available once the **Principal** framework is loaded and you've been assigned a DPE and a plan type.

If part of eVAL is showing as **Pending**, that's normal while setup is in progress — see [Account Status & Access](../getting-started/account-status.md) to understand what each status means.

:::tip Head Principals
If you're a **Head Principal**, you typically also evaluate the assistant principals at your school, in addition to your teachers — but that isn't a requirement. An assistant principal can instead be assigned a **District Principal Evaluator (DPE)**, the same as any principal. Your own evaluation is conducted by a DPE.
:::
