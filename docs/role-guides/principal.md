# Principal Guide

As a principal, you play **two roles** in eVAL at the same time, and you switch between them using [Work Areas](../getting-started/work-areas.md):

- **Evaluator** — you evaluate the teachers at your school (and, if you're a Head Principal, usually the assistant principals too).
- **Evaluatee** — you have your own annual evaluation, conducted by a District Principal Evaluator (DPE).

## Your Responsibilities

| Area | What You Do |
|------|-------------|
| **[Evaluating your teachers](./evaluator.md)** | Conduct observations, collect evidence, and complete their evaluations |
| **[Your own evaluation](./evaluatee.md)** | Self-assessment, artifacts, student growth, and reviewing feedback |
| **[Prompt Bank](../reference/prompts.md)** | Create your own prompts for your teachers' conferences and student growth |
| **[Switching roles](../getting-started/work-areas.md)** | Move between your evaluator and evaluatee work |

## What You Control vs. School & District

Most configuration is handled by your administrators — as a principal, what you control is mainly your own evaluator work:

| Task | You | School Admin | District Admin |
|------|-----|--------------|----------------|
| Conduct your teachers' evaluations | Yes | No | No |
| Create prompts | Yours only | School-wide | District-wide |
| Assign evaluators & plan types | If delegated to your school | If delegated | Yes |
| Override module settings | No | If allowed | Yes |
| Select frameworks | No | No | Yes |

## Two Hats, Two Setups

Each role depends on its own setup being complete, and they're independent:

- You can start evaluating your teachers as soon as the **Teacher** framework is loaded and their evaluations are set up — even if your own evaluation isn't ready yet.
- Your own evaluation becomes available once the **Principal** framework is loaded and you've been assigned a DPE and a plan type.

If part of eVAL is showing as **Pending**, that's normal while setup is in progress — see [Account Status & Access](../getting-started/account-status.md).

:::tip Head Principals
If you're a **Head Principal**, you typically also evaluate the assistant principals at your school, in addition to your teachers — but that isn't a requirement. An assistant principal can instead be assigned a **District Principal Evaluator (DPE)**, the same as any principal. Your own evaluation is conducted by a DPE.
:::
