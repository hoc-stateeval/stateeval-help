# eVAL Roles

*A reference for districts onboarding to eVAL*

Your role in eVAL determines what you can see and do. Some users have a single role; others have multiple roles across different locations. Use this overview to plan which roles your staff will need — these are the same roles your District Security Manager assigns in EDS.

---

## Evaluation Roles

These roles participate directly in evaluations — either being evaluated or conducting evaluations.

| Role | Who They Are | What They Do in eVAL |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Teacher** | Classroom teachers | Participate in their own evaluation — are observed in the classroom, take part in pre/post conferences, submit artifacts, set student growth goals |
| **Principal** | School principals | Evaluate teachers at their school **and** participate in their own evaluation |
| **Head Principal** | Head principals at schools with multiple principals | Evaluate both teachers and assistant principals, plus participate in their own evaluation |
| **District Teacher Evaluator (DTE)** | District-level staff who evaluate teachers | Evaluate teachers who are assigned at the district level (often specialists serving multiple schools) |
| **District Principal Evaluator (DPE)** | District-level staff who evaluate principals | Evaluate head principals |

> **Note — Evaluators are often evaluatees too**
>
> A principal evaluates their teachers, but is also being evaluated by a district principal evaluator. When a person holds both roles, eVAL lets them switch between them.

---

## Administrative Roles

These roles manage the setup and configuration of evaluations.

| Role | Scope | What They Do |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **District Admin** | Entire district | Select frameworks, configure settings, manage assignments, oversee all evaluations |
| **School Admin** | One school | Manage school-level settings and assignments (where the district allows) |
| **District Assignment Manager** | Entire district | Manage evaluator assignments only (a limited admin role) |
| **District Viewer** | Assigned schools | View evaluation progress in read-only mode for oversight |

> **Tip — Start small with admin roles**
>
> A **District Admin** can already do everything a **District Assignment Manager** or **District Viewer** can. Assign those narrower roles only to people who aren't District Admins. Give each person the fewest roles that cover what they actually do, and add more later only if needed.

---

## Multiple Roles and Locations

A single person can hold multiple roles across multiple locations. For example:

- A principal at **two schools** has the same work areas at each school.
- A person who is a **principal at one school and a district admin** switches between evaluation and admin views.

A staff member's annual evaluation is tied to their **district**, not a specific school. Someone who works at multiple schools in the same district has one evaluation for the district.
